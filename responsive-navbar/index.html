I joined an international organization called STEM IS US, where I now lead a team of professional educators. Together, we guide over 300 students from the basics of technology to advanced coding, inspiring those who lack experience and resources to become passionate about coding and create amazing projects. We've taught students from around the world, including China, the US, Canada, Pakistan, India, Turkey, Spain, and the Philippines.

I also collaborate with Project Ivy, a Singapore-based organization that partners with Malaysian and Indonesian tutors to teach English and math, expanding my experience and impact in education.

Not only am I active in international organizations, but I am also deeply involved in national and school organizations. My journey began with the student council in junior high school. I used to be shy and afraid to speak in front of others, but over time, I became more active and confident. Starting as a documentation chairman responsible for documenting the 17th of August event, I gradually moved on to public speaking events with professional coaches. Eventually, I was elected as the head of the student council (OSIS), a role that carries significant responsibility.

I continued to hone my organizational skills by joining groups such as Aspiring Change Makers and DAAI TV. With Aspiring Change Makers, I progressed from a committee member to an HRD officer, helping to provide donations and teach subjects like science, math, and language to underprivileged orphans. I also became the head of the Fed Today Feed Tomorrow program, where I led the initiative and managed finances, starting a business that generated around 1.4 million in profit, benefiting the participants with both money and valuable skills.

At DAAI TV, I had the opportunity to undergo rigorous training as a news caster, much like an apprentice. This hands-on experience allowed me to develop my broadcasting skills significantly, as I participated in various events and learned the intricacies of news casting from seasoned professionals.
